# Understanding How and Why 

## Human Development

- The science of human development studies:
  - **How** people change over time.
  - **Why** people change over time.
- It examines people of all ages, backgrounds, and cultures.

## Scientific Method in Human Development

1. Begin with curiosity and ask questions.
2. Form a **hypothesis** (a testable prediction).
3. Conduct research and gather **empirical evidence**.
4. Draw conclusions based on evidence.
5. Consider biases, alternate explanations, and unexpected results.
6. Report findings and suggest further research.

### Replication

- **Replication** means repeating a study’s procedures with different participants.
- It helps verify whether research findings are reliable and accurate.

## Nature vs. Nurture

- **Nature** = genetic inheritance and biological influences.
- **Nurture** = environmental influences across life, beginning before birth (mother’s health, stress, diet) and continuing through family, school, community, and culture.
- Nature and nurture are **interdependent**:
  - They constantly influence each other.
  - They cannot be divided into fixed percentages that add to 100%.

### Differential Susceptibility

- People differ in how sensitive they are to experiences and environments.
- Development depends on both a person’s biology (“nature”) and environment (“nurture”).
- Some people are like **“dandelions”**: resilient and less affected by surroundings.
- Others are like **“orchids”**: more sensitive, struggling in poor environments but thriving in supportive ones.

## Epigenetics

The study of how environmental factors and experiences can turn genes on or off without changing the DNA itself.
